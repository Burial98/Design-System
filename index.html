Welcome to my modest Design Guide for Websites. It will cover broad aspects and design suggestions for Color, Typography and Images.

This website is in no shape or form exhaustive. It's intended as an inspirational starting point for web and mobile apps.

Color

The importance of Color can't be overstated. It's deeply entrenched with our emotional states and can say so much without actually saying anything. In the following chapter we will look at four common Color Harmony Rules: Monochromatic, Complementary, Analogous and Triadic.

Monochromatic

Monochromatic Color Schemes are created by different tones of one color. After choosing your Base Color, you can vary the saturation and tone to get different hues.

Complementary

Complementary Color Schemes are created by composing two colors opposite on the color wheel. This is the most contrasting of all Color Schemes and can attract much attention when used tastefully.

  • #BA636D

  • #82343D

  • #047D6F

  • #49B3A6

Analogous

In Analogous Color Schemes, the colors grouped have similarities and close relationships to one another. Basically they sit next to each other on the color wheel.

  • #6B8A08

  • #D7A009

  • #CDA565

  • #E8CB61

Triadic

Triadic Color Schemes are made of three colors, which are evenly spaced on the color wheel. You can imagine a triangle sitting on top a color wheel and each corner falls on a specific color.

  • #5C2C9E

  • #FC954C

Typography

Finding well fitting Font-Pairings can be quite the challange. The overwhelming amount of fonts make it hard to choose and if you find a few that fit well together, there will certainly always be another one that would have fitted even better. As a general rule of thumb, we should never choose more than three different fonts for our websites or apps.

The following fonts are all from Google Fonts.
